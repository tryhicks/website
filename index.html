The folder you're afraid to open.

You have a folder. Maybe it's called "Second Brain," "Knowledge Base," or "Zettelkasten." It's full of hundreds of notes that you spent weeks carefully tagging, linking, and organizing. And then you stopped.

Now it's just a graveyard of old ambitions. You don't open it because every time you do, you're reminded of the maintenance you couldn't keep up with. The guilt of the abandoned system is the only thing that's still growing.

Hicks is for the people who love the thinking, but hate the organizing.

One click turns your notes into a mind.

Connect a model — local or cloud, your call. Local keeps everything on your machine. Cloud works too.

Your notes turn into threads. Threads live in pages. Each page is a topic — and every topic holds the thing your notes say together that no single note says alone. Sometimes two of your notes are arguing. That's the contradiction. You see both.

That's how a note gets in: write, talk, or import. Voice sits one click from the mic — and the next build reads it the same as everything else.

Topics. Connections. The contradictions you didn't know were in there.
